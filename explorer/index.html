Did COVID reprice the commute?

Where repricing happened, it favored the periphery — long-commute, low-job-access ZIP-code areas gained rent relative to job-rich cores — and it did not reverse in the return-to-office era. Read this honestly

  • Covered submarket only. Zillow's rent index publishes only where listing volume clears a threshold (70–96% of ZCTAs per metro, far fewer pre-2020); nothing here speaks to uncovered areas.
  • Relative, not causal. Every ZCTA is treated by COVID and there is no control group — estimates are within-metro relative descriptions.
  • “Repricing” = the listing index moved — an amalgam of price and listing-composition change; no hedonic adjustment is possible at this altitude.
  • Joint coefficients are conditional. The three gradients are correlated; where joint and single-interaction models disagree, collinearity is doing the talking. Toggle both above.
  • Memphis is under-identified (12 ZCTAs on both sides of the break) and reads as uninformative, not as a zero.
